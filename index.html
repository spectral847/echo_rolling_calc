Assumptions & modeling notes
  • The 13-substat pool: Crit Rate + Crit Damage (always desired); a subset of 3 secondaries (e.g.: ATK%, Resonance Liberation%, Energy Regen%); the other 5 secondaries (e.g.: Resonance Skill%, Basic Attack%, DEF%, HP%, Heavy Attack%) as undesired; 1 tertiary (e.g.: Flat ATK) as desired; other as undesired tertiaries.
  • Every draw is uniformly random over whatever remains in the 13-slot pool (1/13, 1/12, ... 1/9) — category only determines the value distribution once a substat is picked, matching the Korea revealed values per-substat value tables.
  • A line count score is computed as 2 (CR+CD) + count of desired secondaries + 0.5 if the desired tertiary was rolled. "Target X or better" means this score reaches X — so hitting a 5-liner counts as success for a 4-liner target, etc.
  • Rolling stops (to avoid wasting tuners) the instant the target is locked in, the instant it becomes impossible, on hard invalidation, on an early-stop ditch, or at 5 total rolls (echoes cap at 5 substats).
  • Soft Cull CR/CD rolls: Crit Value = 2×CR% + CD%. The exception is only evaluated once both CR+CD are known (can't compute CV from one value), using the formula as given: CV ≥ 25.2 + 2.4 + 2.4x.
  • Soft Cull secondary rolls: since the 3 desired secondaries have different real units (ATK% vs Energy Regen%, etc.), "average value" is evaluated as the average rank (0=lowest possible roll, 7=highest) across desired-secondary rolls so far, compared against the top of the low band (rank y−1). This keeps the comparison unit-agnostic; treat it as the intended spirit of the rule rather than a literal percentage average.
  • "Failed α liner" is split into three buckets: culled (echo culling triggered), unreachable (bad luck on category draws made the target impossible with no culling involved), and ditched (early-stopping abandoned it). All three cost fewer tuners on average than a full 5-roll echo.
  • Expected echoes/tuners assume each echo attempt is independent (standard geometric-distribution framing): expected echoes = 1/P(success); expected tuners = (10 × average rolls per failed attempt) × (1−p)/p + (10 × average rolls per successful attempt).
  • The "reduction in usable echoes caused by this rule alone" formula: 1 − (expected echoes without this rule ÷ expected echoes with it)
FAQ
  • What's the purpose of this site?
    • Find out how many echoes and tuners you are expected to require for your target echo.
    • Compare early stopping strategies which reduce tuner requirements at the cost of echoes.
    • Visualize how many echoes you are throwing away by refusing low rolls.
  • How is this possible?
    The Korean government forced wuwa to show the susbstat roll probabilities and value probability distribution function. Using this information, probabilities are computed by exhaustively enumerating every possible roll sequence and outcome — not simulated.
  • What is Soft Mode?
    It is only active when culling the last roll. Helps save some decent echoes. e.g.: at +20 you have 10.5% CR and one secondary, +25 rolls 12.6% CD. The calculator will save this echo even if you cull up to the 4th lowest roll. when used in Soft Mode. Hard mode will cull it.
